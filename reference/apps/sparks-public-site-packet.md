## Public Positioning

**App name:** Sparks: Create with AI

**Tagline:** Turn screen time into creation time.

**Short description:**  
Sparks is an AI-powered creative studio where kids build games, art, stories, and interactive projects with help from Sparky, a friendly AI companion.

**Long description:**  
Sparks helps children turn ideas into playable creations. Kids can choose a template, accept a creative challenge, or start from an idea of their own, then collaborate with Sparky through text or voice. Parents get local controls and visibility into activity, creations, and conversation history.

## Target Users

Sparks is built for:

- Children ages 6–12
- Parents and guardians who want more creative screen time
- Families looking for private, ad-free creative play
- Kids who want to make games, art, quizzes, stories, and playful projects without learning formal coding first

## Core Value Proposition

Sparks shifts kids from passive consumption to active creation. Instead of watching another video or playing another finished game, a child can describe an idea and build something playable with AI guidance.

## Public Feature Summary

### AI creative companion

Sparky helps kids brainstorm, customize, and build projects through conversation.

### Templates and creative starters

Kids can start from ready-to-customize templates for games, art, stories, quizzes, and other creative formats.

### Voice and text interaction

Kids can type ideas or use voice input where supported.

### Live preview and play mode

Creations can be previewed and played as they are built.

### Daily creative challenges

Daily Spark challenges encourage a creative habit.

### Family Wall

A private family-focused area lets creations be shared and celebrated without a public social feed.

### Parent Dashboard

Parents can review activity, manage child profiles, set usage limits, adjust content settings, and review conversation history.

### Local-first child profiles

Child profiles and creative activity are stored locally on the device in the current implementation.

### FAQ

**Does Sparks need the internet?**  
AI features such as Sparky chat, voice input, and voice responses require an internet connection. Some app screens may open without internet, but creation features may be limited.

**Who can see my child’s creations?**  
Sparks is designed around private family use. There is no public social feed in the current implementation.

**Does Sparks sync across devices?**  
No. Current data is stored locally on the device. Switching devices or reinstalling the app may result in data loss unless an export feature is available and used.

**What happens if I forget the parent PIN?**  
The parent PIN is stored locally. If there is no recovery flow, reinstalling the app may be required and may erase local data. Confirm current shipped behavior before publishing exact recovery instructions.

**How many child profiles are supported?**  
The current implementation supports a limited number of child profiles per device. Confirm the exact limit before publishing.

**Can parents review conversations?**  
The Parent Dashboard is intended to provide visibility into activity and conversation history.

**Does Sparks have ads or public social features?**  
Do not claim final ad, social, or monetization behavior unless confirmed before publication.

## Privacy Page Inputs

Use cautious privacy language. Do not claim that no data ever leaves the device, because AI-powered chat, voice, and text-to-speech features may transmit content to external providers for processing.

### Privacy positioning

Sparks is designed for parent-supervised creative use by children. Current data storage is local-first, but AI-powered features may transmit conversation text or voice input for processing.

### Data users may enter or create

Sparks may store or process:

- Child display name or profile name
- Child age group or profile settings
- Parent setup information
- Parent PIN stored locally
- Creative projects
- Pixel art or generated project content
- Conversation history with Sparky
- Voice input used for transcription
- Usage minutes and app activity
- Achievements, streaks, and milestones
- Family Wall posts

### Local storage

Profiles, projects, conversation history, activity records, and settings are stored locally on the device in the current implementation.

### Third-party processing

AI features may send conversation text, voice audio, or text-to-speech content to third-party providers for processing. This should be disclosed clearly on the live privacy page after provider details and data-retention terms are confirmed.

### Accounts

Sparks currently does not use a server account system for children. Parent setup is local in the current implementation.
